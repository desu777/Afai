  <FULL_TEXT>Crystal clear aquarium water is the goal of every aquarist, representing not just beauty but a healthy environment for fish and plants. Cloudy, green, or brownish water are common challenges that require practical solutions and proper maintenance routines.

**Why does aquarium water become cloudy?**
- Uneaten food, fish waste, and decaying plant matter increase contamination.
- Disrupted biological balance (often due to new tank syndrome, overstocking, or excessive cleaning) leads to ‘milky water’ caused by bacterial or protozoan blooms.
- Excessive light or high nitrate/phosphate levels trigger algae blooms (green water).
- Improperly prepared substrate or underperforming filtration adds to turbidity.
- Brown water is typically caused by tannins released from driftwood or organic substrate; this is usually safe but can slightly lower pH.

**Proven methods for restoring and maintaining crystal clear water:**
1. **Filtration:** Use a filter with layered mechanical (sponges, floss), biological (volcanic lava, ceramic rings), and chemical (activated carbon, ion exchange resin) media. Clean the filter and its prefilters regularly to ensure effectiveness.
2. **Regular Water Changes:** Perform weekly water changes, replacing 30–60% of the tank’s water to remove built-up contaminants and refresh the ecosystem.
3. **Debris Removal:** Use a gravel vacuum to clean the substrate. Remove decaying plant leaves and other organic debris to prevent excess nutrient buildup. Feed fish only what they will eat in a few minutes to avoid excess food decomposition.
4. **Control Lighting and Fertilization:** Limit lighting to 8–10 hours per day and dose fertilizers according to plant needs. Over-lighting and over-fertilization promote algae blooms. Use reliable test kits (drop-based preferred over strips) to monitor nutrients.
5. **Water Clarifying Treatments:** Use products like AF Clear Boost to bind small suspended particles into larger clumps, making it easier for the filter to trap them and clear up cloudy water quickly.
6. **Dealing with Brown Water:** Activated carbon and ion exchange resin help remove tannins and discoloration caused by driftwood. Patience and time may also be needed.

**Expert tips for prevention and ongoing care:**
- Do not overfeed fish; excess food increases waste and turbidity.
- Maintain glass, substrate, and filter hygiene.
- Preserve biological stability—avoid large, abrupt changes that can upset the beneficial bacteria community.
- Stay patient and consistent; regular care is the most effective method for achieving and maintaining crystal-clear water.

**Summary:** Achieving diamond-clear aquarium water requires a combination of robust filtration, regular maintenance, controlled feeding and fertilization, and the occasional use of water clarifiers.
